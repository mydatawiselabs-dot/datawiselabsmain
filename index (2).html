About Us

A consulting firm built by practitioners

Our Story

DataWise Labs was founded by a group of data professionals who spent years on both sides of the table — as in-house data teams and as consultants — and kept seeing the same problem: organizations were drowning in data but starving for insight.

Every company had spreadsheets, ERP exports, and CRM reports — but no coherent system to connect the dots. Decisions were still being made on gut feel, because the data was either unavailable, unreliable, or incomprehensible to non-technical stakeholders.

We set out to change that. DataWise Labs was built on a simple belief: good data systems should make people smarter, not busier. Every project we take on starts with the question — what decision does this data need to support?

Today, we partner with small and mid-sized businesses across retail, manufacturing, insurance, and CPG to build the data infrastructure, dashboards, and analytics capabilities that make that possible. Our team brings together deep technical skills in data engineering, BI, and cloud platforms with a consulting discipline that keeps every solution anchored to business value.

We're not a software vendor. We're not a staffing agency. We're a focused team of data professionals who take genuine ownership of the problem — and stay accountable to the outcome.

Case Studies

Work that moved the needle

Three examples of how we've helped companies turn raw data into decisions that drove real business outcomes.

Retail

Sales Performance Dashboard

Key Outcome 40% reduction in time spent generating weekly reports
The Problem

Fragmented sales data across 12 stores

A mid-sized retail chain had store-level data spread across multiple POS systems and Excel files. Weekly sales reports took two days to compile and were always at least 48 hours stale by the time leadership saw them.

Our Solution

Unified data pipeline + Power BI dashboard

We built an automated ETL pipeline that consolidated all store-level POS data into a central data warehouse. On top of that, we designed a live Power BI dashboard with store-level, regional, and product-category views.

The Outcome

Real-time visibility, faster decisions

Leadership now had a live dashboard refreshed every 2 hours. Weekly report generation dropped from 2 days to zero. The team identified a 15% sales uplift opportunity in a previously invisible product category within the first month.

Manufacturing

Production & OEE Analytics

Key Outcome 12% improvement in Overall Equipment Effectiveness
The Problem

No visibility into machine downtime causes

A component manufacturer was losing significant production time to unplanned downtime but had no systematic way to track causes, duration, or impact by shift, machine, or product line.

Our Solution

MES data integration + OEE tracking system

We integrated data from their Manufacturing Execution System and floor-level logs into a structured analytics database. We then built shift-level OEE dashboards and a downtime cause classification system to surface patterns.

The Outcome

Data-driven maintenance decisions

Within three months, the operations team identified three recurring failure modes responsible for 70% of unplanned downtime. Targeted maintenance schedules improved OEE by 12%, recovering over 300 production hours per quarter.

Insurance

Claims Analytics Platform

Key Outcome 30% faster claims trend identification
The Problem

Claims data locked in legacy systems

A regional insurance firm was unable to detect early warning signals in claims trends because data lived in a legacy system with no reporting capability. Actuarial analyses were done quarterly, too slow to inform pricing decisions.

Our Solution

Data extraction + analytics dashboard

We built a secure data extraction process from their legacy claims system into a modern data warehouse. On top of it, we designed a claims analytics dashboard covering frequency, severity, product line, and geography — updated monthly.

The Outcome

Faster, more confident underwriting

The actuarial and underwriting teams could now see claims trends monthly instead of quarterly. The firm identified a deteriorating claims pattern in one product segment four months earlier than they would have previously, enabling a proactive pricing correction.
